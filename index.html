SLHS Computer Science Club









Hello, World!

We're the Seven Lakes High School Computer Science Club. Our goal is to provide a place for students interested in computer science to connect, develop their skills, and serve their school. We host weekly presentations, delivered by our own members, and mutliple events throughout the year from practice sessions to socials. Our members can explore their interests in different areas of computer science, compete in local and national contests, and distingush themselves by joining the national CS Honor Society.

We meet every Monday at 2:45 P.M. in Room 1001.

Frequently Asked Questions

What activities does the club do?

We hold presentations on computer science topics every week, practice for contests, host a national contest, and provide an environment for Seven Lakes students interested in computer science to connect with each other.

How do I join?

Our club is open to anyone who wants to attend our meetings. No sign up is required and we don't keep a list of members. As far as we're concerned, if you come to meetings, you're part of the club.

Do I have to pay any fees?

No! Our club is completely free to everyone. If you meet the requirements and would like to join National CS Honor Society after a year, then we ask for a small fee to cover the cost of the Honor Society.

Do I need to compete to be in the club?

Competitions are not required. We encourage everyone to give contests a try because they're fun and a great way to improve your problem solving skills, but we recognize that there wide range of interests in CS beyond competitions.

Contests

Pizza. Candy. Code. We attend several local computer science contests throughout the year hosted by neighboring schools. Teams of 3 program on one computer to complete a packet of programming problems (while eating pizza and candy, of course). They're a fun way to spend a Saturday and improve your problem solving skills. Our other contests include USACO, HP Code Wars, and UIL CS, where we frequently advance to state.


We hold competitive programming practice every week.


We host a national contest. Every year our annual October contest is hosted online and in-person with over 350 people in attendance. The contest is produced entirely by the CS Club and every year we require problem writers, testers, judges, and other volunteers to help put the event together.

Opportunities

Join a National Organization

CSHS (Computer Science Honor Society) is a prestigious national organization that recognizes outstanding achievement in computer science. By joining CSHS, you'll become part of a community of high-achieving students across the country.

Benefits of CSHS Membership:

  • National recognition for your CS achievements
  • Enhanced college applications and resumes
  • Access to exclusive scholarships and opportunities
  • Networking with other high-achieving CS students
  • Leadership development opportunities

Requirements for Induction: To be inducted into CSHS, you need to volunteer 8 hours towards our club activities, pay the membership fee through Pay n Go under Computer Science Club, and fill out the official Google form. This is your chance to distinguish yourself in the CS community and show your dedication to excellence.

Be a Leader in Your Community

Leadership opportunities are abundant in our CS Club, allowing you to develop essential skills while helping others grow in their computer science journey.

Leadership Roles Available:

  • Student Presenters: Lead weekly presentations on CS topics
  • Peer Tutors: Help other students with programming concepts
  • Event Organizers: Plan and coordinate club activities
  • Contest Coordinators: Help organize programming competitions
  • Mentors: Guide newer members in their CS journey

Why Lead? Taking on leadership roles helps you develop communication skills, build confidence, and create a positive impact on your peers. It's also excellent preparation for college and future careers in technology. Our club provides a supportive environment where you can learn to lead while pursuing your passion for computer science.

Build Your Portfolio

Real-world projects are the best way to showcase your programming skills and creativity. Our club provides numerous opportunities to work on meaningful projects that serve the community and enhance your technical portfolio.

Project Opportunities:

  • Web Development: Create websites for school organizations
  • Mobile Apps: Develop apps to solve real problems
  • Data Analysis: Work with real datasets and create visualizations
  • Automation Tools: Build tools to streamline school processes
  • Open Source: Contribute to existing projects and learn collaboration

Portfolio Impact: These projects demonstrate your practical skills, problem-solving abilities, and commitment to using technology for good. They're perfect for college applications, job interviews, and showcasing your growth as a programmer. Plus, you'll have the satisfaction of creating something that actually helps people!
